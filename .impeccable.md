## Design Context

### Users

Shopify store owners, international/English-speaking. Small-to-mid-size merchants ($1K-$10K/mo). They're browsing for apps during work hours, comparing options, evaluating trust and value. They've been burned by overpriced apps that promise too much. They want tools that do one thing well at a fair price.

### Brand Personality

**Calm, reliable, clear.** Foxry is a small independent studio — not a VC-backed startup, not a faceless corporation. The tone is confident without being loud. Like a craftsperson who lets the work speak. Direct, honest, no marketing fluff.

### Aesthetic Direction

- **Bold, conversion-focused** — big typography, clear hierarchy, direct messaging
- **International/Western SaaS feel** — spacious, confident, not compact or decorative
- **Light theme** — merchants browsing during work hours. Logo is transparent PNG requiring white/light header area
- **Warm palette** — beige background (#f5f3ef), orange accent (#e8601c). Can be refined but keep the warm direction
- Anti-references: generic AI-generated sites, Japanese-minimal style, dark mode with neon accents, overly decorative/illustrative

### Design Principles

1. **Say it big, say it once.** Headlines should make statements. No redundant explanations.
2. **Earn trust through clarity.** Show pricing, show the product, show what it does. No hidden information.
3. **Products are the hero.** Screenshots and features do the selling, not marketing fluff.
4. **Affordable is a strength.** Don't apologize for low prices — frame them as a deliberate choice.
5. **One studio, multiple tools.** Each product has its own identity within the Foxry family.
